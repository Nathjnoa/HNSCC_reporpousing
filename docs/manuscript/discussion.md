## Interpretive seeds to develop

- **Metabolic reprogramming (Warburg-like shift).** The strongest down-regulated Hallmark
  signature was oxidative phosphorylation (NES = −2.21), consistent with a glycolytic
  metabolic shift in the HNSCC tumor compartment. Develop in the context of the OXPHOS /
  Complex I findings and the metformin nomination (metabolic vulnerability, combination
  therapy rationale).

- **Adjacent-normal tissue composition.** The most extreme under-abundant proteins
  (skeletal/cardiac muscle: MYH2, MYL1/2/3, CASQ1, TCAP, ATP2A1; airway/secretory: BPIFA1,
  BPIFB1) reflect the muscle- and mucosa-rich composition of adjacent-normal tongue and larynx
  specimens rather than tumor-intrinsic biology. Frame as a limitation addressed by the tissue-
  composition sensitivity analysis (prioritized targets and OXPHOS axis robust to exclusion).

- **EGFR recovery as positive control supports novel nominations.** EGFR is the principal
  molecular target with established approved therapy in HNSCC; the unsupervised recovery of
  cetuximab and other approved EGFR-directed agents at the top of the ranking acts as an
  internal positive control and, by extension, supports the credibility of the method's novel
  nominations (metformin/Complex I, proteasome, epigenetic regulators).
